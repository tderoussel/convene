# Convene — Strategic Recommendations

## Executive Summary

Based on competitive research, algorithm design analysis, community growth studies, and landing page best practices, here are the top strategic recommendations for Convene.

## Top 10 Recommendations

### 1. Position as a daily-use product, not a community
"The curated daily feed for founders" — competition is the morning LinkedIn scroll, not Hampton's annual membership. The product should create a daily habit, not a periodic check-in.

### 2. Launch with 150-200 hand-picked founding members
Offer Founding Member status with permanent perks (free premium, badge, product input). Do not open public applications until this core is active and generating content.

### 3. Accept ~30% of applicants at launch, tighten over time
Announce acceptance rate publicly. At 500 members → 25%. At 2,000 → 15-20%. Each tightening is a marketing moment.

### 4. Show 5 curated profiles per day
3 algorithmic matches + 1 serendipitous + 1 featured. This is the core daily habit loop. The scarcity is intentional (Hinge model).

### 5. Free base tier + $29/mo Pro + $99/mo Patron
The application is the gate, not the price. Free base maximizes network density. Paid tiers monetize power users who want more matches, DM access, and hosting privileges.

### 6. Do NOT implement one-way DM restrictions
Tier-based messaging restrictions create a caste system that kills community belonging. Instead: rate-limit DMs for everyone, let recipients set preferences, require connection request with a note.

### 7. Rooms should have structure and limits
Max 30 members per room. Consider time-bounded rooms (7-day lifespan) for urgency. Require a contribution to enter (prevents lurking). Room hosts earn reputation.

### 8. Notification discipline
One daily push at 8 AM ("Your matches are ready") + one weekly digest on Monday. Max 8 notifications per week. Never exceed. Never guilt-trip.

### 9. Dark-mode design with surgical accent color
Deep near-black background, Inter font, no gradients, no glass morphism. Let typography and whitespace do the work. The design should signal "Linear for networking."

### 10. Web-first, mobile-responsive
Build native mobile at 2,000+ active members. The daily-batch model fits desktop morning routines. Mobile-web must work perfectly from day 1.

## Potential Pivots to Consider

### Tier Naming
Current Explorer/Builder/Catalyst is decent but consider Member/Pro/Patron for clearer value signaling. "Explorer" sounds uncommitted; "Patron" signals community investment.

### Pricing Adjustment
$19-29/mo range is fine for Pro. But consider $99/mo Patron tier to capture established founders. The spread creates a natural upgrade path.

### Application Process
Current application is good but should ask real business metrics (revenue range, team size, funding stage) not just culture questions. This is what Hampton does and it builds trust in network quality.

### Content Strategy for Growth
The founding team needs a content presence (Twitter/X, newsletter) to drive qualified applications. Every successful exclusive community (Hampton, Chief, On Deck) had a founder with distribution. If Convene's founders don't have this, they need to build it or partner with someone who does.
